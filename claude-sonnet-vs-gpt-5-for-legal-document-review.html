Claude Sonnet vs GPT-5 for Legal Document Review

Legal document review remains one of the most time-intensive and expensive aspects of law practice. Whether you're managing due diligence, contract analysis, or compliance checks, the ability to accurately process hundreds of pages quickly can dramatically impact your bottom line. AI language models like Claude Sonnet and GPT-5 have emerged as powerful tools to automate this workflow, but they offer different strengths and limitations that directly affect legal accuracy and efficiency.

Choosing between Claude Sonnet and GPT-5 isn't straightforward because legal work demands both precision and contextual understanding. Each model brings distinct advantages: Claude Sonnet excels at nuanced analysis and explicit reasoning, while GPT-5 offers broader training data and faster processing speeds. Your choice ultimately depends on your specific legal use cases, document complexity, and integration requirements.

This guide examines how these two AI systems compare across critical dimensions for legal document review, helping you make an informed decision for your practice.

Model Architecture and Legal Training

Claude Sonnet's Design Philosophy

Claude Sonnet was built with constitutional AI principles and explicit reasoning transparency. The model tends toward careful analysis of contractual language and uses structured thinking to work through complex legal provisions. Anthropic trained Claude on diverse legal materials with an emphasis on reducing hallucinations—a critical concern when reviewing contracts worth millions.

Key architectural advantages for legal work:

  • **Longer context windows** (200K tokens) allowing review of substantial contracts in single requests
  • **Chain-of-thought reasoning** that shows its analysis process
  • **Constitutional AI training** designed to reduce confident false statements

GPT-5's Capabilities and Scale

GPT-5 represents OpenAI's latest generation with significantly expanded model capacity and training data. The model processes information at scale and excels at identifying patterns across diverse document types. GPT-5 was trained on broader datasets, potentially including more recent legal precedents and regulatory updates through its training cutoff.

Distinguishing features for legal applications:

  • **Massive parameter count** enabling nuanced understanding of legal terminology
  • **Faster inference speeds** for processing high document volumes
  • **Broader pattern recognition** across different legal domains and jurisdictions

Accuracy and Hallucination Rates

Factual Consistency in Contract Review

Claude Sonnet demonstrates lower hallucination rates in legal document analysis, particularly when reviewing unfamiliar contract templates or obscure clauses. The model's explicit reasoning process means you can audit why it reached specific conclusions about contractual obligations.

When reviewing a 50-page M&A agreement, Claude Sonnet typically:

  • Accurately identifies all material representations and warranties
  • Flags ambiguous language with supporting quotes
  • Maintains consistency across related provisions
  • Explicitly states when information is insufficient

GPT-5's Performance Trade-offs

GPT-5 sometimes generates plausible-sounding but incorrect interpretations of legal language, particularly with archaic or specialized terminology. However, its broader training means it often recognizes legal references and precedents that Claude Sonnet might miss.

Common differences in output:

  • More confident tone regardless of certainty level
  • Better pattern matching across disparate contract sections
  • Occasional creative but legally inaccurate summaries
  • Superior performance on regulatory language analysis

Integration and Workflow Capabilities

Claude Sonnet Implementation

Claude Sonnet integrates well with document management systems through API connections and handles structured data extraction effectively. The model works particularly well with workflows requiring:

  • **Batch processing** of similar documents with consistent parameters
  • **Custom instructions** that remain consistent across requests
  • **JSON-formatted outputs** for downstream database integration
  • **Audit trails** showing reasoning for compliance documentation
Practical implementation: A mid-size firm processes vendor contracts through Claude Sonnet's API, receiving standardized JSON outputs containing identified risks, payment terms, and renewal clauses.

GPT-5 Integration Advantages

GPT-5 offers broader integration options through multiple platforms and interfaces. It handles variable document formats and less-structured inputs more flexibly. The model performs better when legal documents arrive in mixed formats (PDFs, Word, scanned images) without preprocessing.

Notable integration features:

  • Flexible input handling with automatic OCR quality detection
  • Broader third-party plugin ecosystem
  • Faster processing for high-volume scenarios
  • Better performance with cross-jurisdictional documents

Cost and Efficiency Considerations

Pricing Models

Claude Sonnet operates on per-token pricing ($3 per 1M input tokens, $15 per 1M output tokens). For a 100-page document averaging 400 tokens per page, expect roughly $0.12-0.18 per review depending on analysis depth.

GPT-5 pricing through OpenAI's API starts higher but offers volume discounts. Processing identical 100-page documents costs approximately $0.25-0.35, though speed improvements may offset cost differences.

Time-to-Review Analysis

Claude Sonnet typically requires 8-12 seconds to analyze a 50-page contract with detailed reasoning, while GPT-5 completes the same task in 4-6 seconds. For document volumes under 100 per month, this difference has minimal impact. For practices processing 500+ documents monthly, GPT-5's speed advantage becomes significant.

ROI Calculations

A 5-attorney firm reviewing 300 vendor contracts monthly:

  • Manual review time: 20 hours (6-7 minutes per contract)
  • Claude Sonnet implementation: 4 hours for AI review + 1.5 hours lawyer validation
  • Annual labor savings: ~$120,000
  • Annual AI costs: ~$2,160

Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can either model replace human legal review entirely?**

A: No. Both models excel at first-pass analysis, flagging potential issues, and organizing documents. However, final review by qualified attorneys remains essential, especially for high-stakes transactions. Use AI as a force multiplier for paralegal-level work rather than attorney replacement.

**Q: Which model handles cross-jurisdictional documents better?**

A: GPT-5 generally performs better with documents involving multiple jurisdictions due to its broader training data on regional legal variations. Claude Sonnet is superior when you need detailed reasoning about specific jurisdictional requirements.

**Q: How do these models handle proprietary or confidential information?**

A: Both offer API options allowing on-premises deployment or private cloud instances. Claude offers SOC 2 compliance, while GPT-5 through OpenAI Enterprise provides enhanced data privacy. Never input confidential information through free web interfaces.

**Q: What's the learning curve for implementation?**

A: Claude Sonnet integration requires minimal setup (typically 2-4 hours for basic implementation). GPT-5 through various platforms ranges from plug-and-play to more complex custom configurations (4-16 hours depending on workflow depth).

**Q: Which model is better for contract comparison and redline analysis?**

A: Claude Sonnet edges ahead for detailed redline explanations and provision-by-provision comparison. Its longer context window accommodates multiple full contracts simultaneously. GPT-5 is faster but sometimes overlooks subtle differences between similar clauses.

Conclusion

Claude Sonnet and GPT-5 represent different philosophical approaches to legal AI. Claude Sonnet prioritizes accuracy, transparency, and explainability—ideal for firms requiring detailed reasoning trails and handling complex, specialized legal analysis. GPT-5 prioritizes speed, pattern recognition, and integration flexibility—better suited for high-volume document processing across diverse legal domains.

Your decision should align with operational priorities. Choose Claude Sonnet if you process fewer documents but require bulletproof accuracy and detailed reasoning. Choose GPT-5 if you handle large volumes and need rapid throughput with flexible integrations.

Realistically, forward-thinking legal practices will likely deploy both models strategically: Claude Sonnet for high-stakes, complex analysis and GPT-5 for volume-based screening and initial document classification. This hybrid approach maximizes efficiency while maintaining the accuracy standards legal work demands.

Start with pilot programs using your actual documents before committing to either solution. The best choice depends on your specific workflow, not abstract model comparisons.
