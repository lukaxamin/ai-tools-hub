Step 1: Defining Your Episode Concept

Starting with a Clear Topic

Begin by telling Claude your episode's core topic and intended audience. The more specific you are, the better Claude understands your needs.

**Example prompt:** > "I host a business strategy podcast aimed at early-stage founders. My next episode will explore bootstrapping versus venture capital funding. My audience is 25-40 year old entrepreneurs making funding decisions. Create a podcast outline that feels conversational but authoritative, running about 45 minutes."

Clarifying Your Angle

Claude needs to understand your unique perspective. Specify:

  • Your personal experience or expertise with the topic
  • Controversial angles or unconventional takes
  • Stories or case studies you want to feature
  • Guest insights (if applicable)
  • Actionable takeaways for listeners
Providing this context ensures Claude doesn't generate generic outlines. Instead, it creates structure tailored to your voice and expertise.

Step 2: Generating Structured Outlines

The Three-Act Outline Format

Claude can organize episodes using the classic storytelling structure: setup, development, payoff. This format works across all podcast genres.

**Example prompt:** > "Structure the funding episode in three acts: (1) Why founders face this decision, (2) bootstrapping benefits and challenges, (3) VC funding trade-offs and when each makes sense. Include specific talking points and transitions between each act."

Claude will provide:

  • Opening hook (15-30 seconds)
  • Setup section with context-setting
  • Development with main arguments and counterarguments
  • Resolution with actionable guidance
  • Closing statement reinforcing key message

Segmented Outline Format

For longer episodes, break content into distinct segments with time allocations.

**Example structure Claude creates:**

  • **Intro & Hook** (2 min): Attention-grabbing question or story
  • **Context** (8 min): Historical background and why this matters now
  • **Main Argument #1** (10 min): Primary point with supporting evidence
  • **Main Argument #2** (10 min): Secondary point with examples
  • **Listener Challenge** (5 min): Actionable step listeners can take immediately
  • **Outro** (2 min): Summary and call-to-action
This format keeps you on track during recording and prevents episodes from meandering.

Step 3: Developing Compelling Content Within Your Outline

Creating Engaging Hooks

Your opening 30 seconds determine if listeners continue. Ask Claude to generate multiple opening hooks for your topic.

**Example prompt:** > "Generate 5 different opening hooks for an episode about founder burnout. Make them provocative but authentic. Include one based on a startling statistic, one on a personal confession, one on a question, one on a counterintuitive claim, and one on a relevant quote."

Claude provides options you can test, giving you several entry points to choose from based on what feels most authentic to your delivery style.

Building Narrative Arcs Within Segments

Even within individual segments, Claude helps create mini-narratives with setup, conflict, and resolution.

**Example:** For a segment about founder burnout, Claude might structure:

  • Setup: Describe a relatable burnout scenario
  • Conflict: Explain why this happens and why typical solutions fail
  • Resolution: Offer specific, implementable strategies
This approach prevents segments from feeling like lists of facts and instead maintains listener engagement throughout.

Step 4: Incorporating Guest and Interview Elements

Preparing for Guest Episodes

When featuring guests, Claude helps create an outline that showcases them effectively while maintaining episode momentum.

**Example prompt:** > "I'm interviewing a founder who bootstrapped to $2M revenue. Create an outline for a 60-minute interview episode. Include 8-10 interview questions ordered to build narrative tension. Start with their background, move to the key decision points, then explore lessons learned. Leave space for organic conversation."

Claude structures interviews to feel natural rather than scripted, suggesting question sequencing that lets conversations develop.

Question Development

Claude can generate follow-up questions and probing questions that go beyond surface-level responses.

**Standard questions to ask Claude:**

  • "What are 5 follow-up questions I should ask if the guest mentions challenges with scaling?"
  • "Create 3 questions that challenge conventional wisdom about this topic"
  • "Suggest difficult but fair questions that might make the guest pause and think deeply"

Step 5: Optimizing for Different Podcast Formats

Solo Commentary Episodes

For single-host shows, Claude structures content to feel like a natural discussion despite the monologue format.

Effective solo outlines include:

  • Rhetorical questions posed to listeners
  • Anticipated counterarguments you address
  • Personal anecdotes breaking up exposition
  • Direct listener calls-to-action

Interview-Focused Podcasts

Interview outlines emphasize questions while suggesting transitions and theme connections.

Narrative/Storytelling Podcasts

Claude helps structure plot points, character development, and reveals to maintain suspense across episodes.

Step 6: Refining and Iterating Your Outline

Testing Different Structures

Don't settle on Claude's first output. Experiment with variations.

**Iteration prompt:** > "You gave me this outline, but I'm concerned it feels too linear. Can you restructure it using a problem-solution framework instead? Start with the listener's pain point, then reveal your perspective."

Time Testing

Before recording, read your outline aloud while timing yourself. Use Claude to adjust:

**Example prompt:** > "My outline is running 42 minutes but I need exactly 45 minutes. Where should I expand for more depth without adding filler?"

Claude suggests high-impact areas worth elaborating rather than padding weak sections.

Extracting Key Soundbites

Ask Claude to identify the most quotable lines or memorable phrases that could work as social media content.
